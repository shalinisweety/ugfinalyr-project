Overview

Siruvani was built for Tamil Nadu by the Kerala government with funds gathered by the Tamil Nadu government to meet the drinking water necessities of Coimbatore. The gateways on either side of the road across the dam are typically in Kerala and Tamil Nadu architectural styles.

Siruvani Waterfalls and the dam named after them have situated 36 km (22 mi) west of Coimbatore in the Western Ghats. Also located at 50 Km distance from Palakkad & 535 Kms from Chennai. It is also known as Kovai Courtallam

Additionally, Siruvani is home to certain Tribals like the Mudugars and Irulars. Water from Siruvani River is famous for taste and its mineral properties. It is known to be the 2nd sweetest lake water in the world. The sweetness has been credited to the vegetation & rocks through which the water streams in the Attappady areas.

An almost 1 km of trek-walk through the forest is required to reach exact spot for bathing. The upper part of the falls is very dangerous with steep rock and the tourists are usually enjoying the beauty and bathing at the bottom of the falls only.

Best Season

 The best time to visit Siruvani Falls is between July and early September, during the monsoon season.

Trek length

 Siruvani falls can be reached by about 4 Kms trek from the nearest road point between Siruvani Dam and Madavarayapuram.

Distance

 At a distance of 36 Kms from Coimbatore Railway Station, 50 Kms from Palakkad & 535 Kms from Chennai

Difficulty Level

 Since the difficulty level of treks is easy to moderate,it is a great place for beginners to experience the adrenaline rush while trekking uphill.
